ⓘ How pairing logic works

This guide explains score groups, natural opponents, floaters, color due, and illegal matchups. Ratings seed players and break ties; current score remains the main pairing priority.

Top-half players sit on the left and bottom-half players on the right. A changed opponent means the engine had to depart from a natural pairing to solve a restriction.

Pairing order

  1. Requested byes: players who requested the round sit out first and receive ½ point.
  2. Score groups: remaining players are grouped by current score, highest group first.
  3. Floaters: an odd score group sends its lowest seed down one score group so everyone can be paired.
  4. Illegal matchups: the engine backtracks to avoid a previous opponent. A rematch is used only if no rematch-free arrangement is available.
  5. Full bye: if the eligible field is still odd, the player with the fewest prior full byes is preferred; ties go to the lower score, then lower rating.

Color due (W / WW / B / BB)

WW or BB: third consecutive color would occur→Strongest: give the opposite color W or B: White–Black balance is unequal→Next: equalize the color count Balance equal and no severe conflict→Alternate last color; then use a deterministic fallback

W/B means due White/Black for equalization; WW/BB marks the stronger consecutive-color issue. A balance of −1 means one more Black than White. Byes do not count as a color.

Family, siblings, teams, and other protected pairings

Use the Family / sibling groups card below to mark players who shouldn't meet — siblings, parent/child, spouses, or any other group. It's treated as a soft rule, the same way rematches are: avoided whenever an alternative pairing exists, and only allowed if the round genuinely can't be paired evenly otherwise. Team/school/club exclusions beyond marked groups aren't collected, so review the generated boards and use “Fix a mistake in this round” if you need a manual swap for something the engine doesn't know about.

Pairings are deterministic for the same roster and entered results. Manual swaps change the displayed pairing but do not add a permanent exclusion rule for later rounds.
