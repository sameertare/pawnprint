What this is

OpenFile is a hub of eight small, focused chess tools covering the full arc of competitive improvement: understanding your own games, training against an engine, preparing an opening repertoire, running a tournament, and tracking a rating. Each is its own page, reachable from the home hub — a player, a coach, and a tournament director each land on the page built for them, rather than one sprawling app to learn.

Everything — including a full chess engine — runs in the browser. Game analysis uses Stockfish 18 (lite) compiled to WebAssembly and executed on your own machine; your PGNs and reports are never uploaded anywhere. Live games stream directly from the public lichess API straight to your browser, with no OpenFile server in that path. The only feature that touches a backend at all is optional server-side report save/load, off by default on the static-hosted version of the site.

The eight tools

For the player

ToolWhat it's for
Performance AnalysisA full diagnostic report from your chess.com / lichess games — opening strengths and weaknesses, phase-by-phase accuracy, tactical misses, time-trouble patterns, head-to-head records, and a prioritized training plan. Reports save as portable files that grow over time, and export to PDF.
Live & EngineOne board, three modes: analyze any position, play a full game against the engine, or follow a live lichess game move-by-move with feedback on every move played. Endgames with few pieces left get an instant tablebase-perfect verdict alongside Stockfish's own evaluation.
Opening ExplorerTurns game history — yours, or an upcoming opponent's — into a browsable opening tree with win rates at every branch, then drills your repertoire with spaced repetition so weak lines surface more often until they stick.

For the coach & tournament director

ToolWhat it's for
Swiss PairingsRuns a full Swiss tournament from a roster — automatic pairings, bye handling, sibling/family conflict avoidance, live standings with official tiebreaks, wall charts, and a full explanation behind every pairing decision.
Coach RosterA dashboard for tracking a whole roster of students at once — score, accuracy, and weakest opening per player, sorted so whoever needs attention next rises to the top.
Compare ReportsPuts two saved reports side by side — one player's progress over time, or a head-to-head scouting comparison — with every metric delta highlighted.

Rating utilities

ToolWhat it's for
USCF Rating EstimatorPredicts a post-event US Chess rating using the official published formula, ahead of the federation's own results.
FIDE Rating EstimatorThe same estimate for FIDE Standard ratings, using FIDE's own K-factor rules.

How it's built

  • Private by construction. Game data and the chess engine both run on-device. Nothing is uploaded unless you turn on optional cloud report storage.
  • Zero marginal infrastructure. Ships as a static site. No server, database, or hosting bill is required to run any core tool.
  • Installs like a native app. A Progressive Web App — add it to a home screen, and keep analyzing games with no connection at all once the engine has loaded, useful in a tournament hall with bad wifi. Live game streaming and account lookups still need a connection, since those come from lichess's/chess.com's public APIs.
  • No account, no paywall. Every tool is free and open the moment the page loads — nothing to sign up for before getting value.

Development note

The current feature set — tablebase lookups, PDF export, opponent scouting reports, the coach dashboard, and the spaced-repetition opening trainer — shipped in a single focused push. A photo-based tournament-results reader was also built and tested, then removed after real-world photos (handwritten sheets, dense multi-column wall charts) proved unreliable to parse — a deliberate call to cut a feature that didn't clear the bar rather than ship something flaky.
